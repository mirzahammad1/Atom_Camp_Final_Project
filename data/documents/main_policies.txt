STUDENT GENERAL DISCIPLINE RULES : PAF-KIET
Introduction
1. These rules shall be called the “PAF-KIET Student General Discipline Rules”.
These rules define moral code and ethics pertaining to the students. These rules shall
have jurisdiction for all PAF-KIET campuses. The details are given below.
Terminologies
2. Unless otherwise explained in the context or explicitly expressed, the following
terms shall mean as defined in each case:-
“Department” means a department of PAF-KIET.
“Campus” means campus of PAF-KIET.
“Committee” means the Students Discipline Committee of PAF-KIET constituted under
these rules.
“Constituent College” means a constituent college of the PAF-KIET.
“Dean” means Dean of respective faculty of PAF-KIET.
“Director Students Affair” means Director Students Affairs of PAF-KIET
“Examination Hall” means the centre for holding examinations.
“Head” means the head of a teaching department.
“Students” mean bona fide students of PAF-KIET both native and foreign in accordance
with the respective rules.
“Institute” means PAF-KIET.
“President” and other officers/authorities mean the President and other officers/
authorities of PAF-KIET.
Code of Honor
3. Every student of the Institute is an honorable student and must observe the
following code of honor:-
a) He must respect the conviction of others in matters of religion, custom and
tradition.
b) He must refrain from doing anything which lowers the honor and prestige of
Pakistan.
c) He must be truthful and honest in his dealings with others.
d) He must respect the elders, especially women, children, old people, etc.
e) He must respect his teachers and others in authority in the Institute.
f) He must devote himself to the studies.
g) He must protect his and others property and the property of the Institute.
Code of Conduct
4. No student shall:-
a) Smoke at the Institute campuses.
b) Consume alcoholic liquor or other intoxicating drugs within the Institute
campuses or during the sports or cultural tours or survey-camp, or enter any such
tour or camp, while under the influence of such intoxicant.
c) Visit other campuses of Institute without permission, or without any academic
or official requirement.
d) Organize or take part in any function within the Institute campuses or
organize any club or society of students except in accordance with the prescribed
rules and regulations, or organize any gathering or address any gathering, without
permission of authorized person.
e) Create, represent or join any political or religious group, being identified as
Institute political or religious group.
f) Collect any money or receive donations or pecuniary assistance for or on
behalf of the Institute or any of the Institute organization except with the written
permission of the Institute authorities.
g) Stage, incite, participate in or indulge in any walkout, strike or other form of
agitation against the Institute or its teachers or officers.
h) Interfere in the official proceedings of the examination or other business.
i) Threatens or misbehaves with any teacher, officers or other employees of
the Institute or try to influence such officers or employees in any way in connection
with their official assignments.
j) Instigate or take part in any boycott of examination or create disturbance in
or around the examination hall.
k) Defame name of Institute or any college/department of the Institute or
Institute management/faculty/employees or their families, or student of the Institute,
by using electronic media or print media or social media.
l) Create any page or ID that resembles any of the Institute official page(s), IDs
or sites.
m) Post or display images or texts which defame name of Institute, Institute
management/faculty/staff members or their families, or students of the Institute.
n) Carry out propaganda against higher management, faculty member, officer
or staff member of the Institute, or student of the Institute or against any policy of
the Institute.
o) Involve or inform non-concerned people regarding Institute administrative or
policy matters, which may defame name of the Institute.
p) Abuse computer facilities and resources including, but not limited to:
i) Use of other individual’s identification and / or password.
ii) Interference with the work of another student, faculty member, Institute
official.
iii) Unauthorized entry into a file to use, read, or change the contents or for
any other purpose.
iv) Unauthorized transfer of a file.
v) Sending obscene, abusive or threatening messages.
vi) Transmission of computer virus.
vii) Unauthorized access to, or unauthorized or malicious use of computer
equipment or networks, or electronic communication devices, or the use of
such equipment or devices to gain unauthorized access to, and / or use of,
off-campuses computer equipment.
viii) Unauthorized duplication of software or other violation of copyright laws.
ix) Interfering with normal operation of the Institute computing system.
x) Indulging in cyber crime.
q) Make unauthorized access or entry to, or use of Institute facilities and equipment,
unauthorized possession, duplication or use of keys to any premises, facilities or
equipment.
r) Intentionally and/or recklessly endangering, threatening, physical or verbal coercion,
harassment, physical harm to any person, or oneself, in Institute premises or at Institute
sponsored activities, using words or actions to provoke or encourage others to violent
retaliatory behavior, or other acts of misconduct.
s) Indulge in any theft, or attempted theft of property or services in Institute premises
or at Institute -sponsored activities.
t) Intentionally and/or recklessly destroying or damaging, or attempting to destroy or
damage Institute property or the property of others in Institute premises or at Institute -
sponsored activities.
u) Indulge in any unauthorized and / or illegal exchange of money, favors or services
as a result of an organized or unorganized game or competition.
v) Perform kissing, hugging, inappropriate touching, etc.
w) Use mobile phone in class rooms, examination halls, labs and library thus disrupting
the calm of the said rooms.
Authorities/Responsibilities of Faculty Members/Staff Members
5. Following are authorities/responsibilities of Faculty Members/Staff Members:-
a) Every member of the teaching staff shall have the powers (and it shall be his
duty) to check disorderly or improper conduct or any breach of the rules by students
occurring in any part of the Institute. Should such misconduct occur in a class-room
when the student is under the charge of any instructor/supervisor, the latter shall
report the matter, without delay, to the College Director/Campus Director/HoD.
b) The Librarian shall be responsible for maintenance of order in the library. In
case of disorderly conduct or any breach of rules, he/she may require the student
so offending to withdraw from the library for the remainder of the day and shall
immediately report the offense to the Campus Director/Director Admin/authorized
person.
c) The Director Physical Education/Head of Sports shall be responsible for the
maintenance of order among the students on or near the playground or while
otherwise under his charge.
ID Card/Visitor Card
6. Rules regarding ID Card/Visitor Card and permissions to attend activities are given
below:-
a) Each student of the Institute is issued with a Identification /Registration card
or visitor’s pass, and must wear it at all times while in Institute premises or at
Institute-sponsored activities. Identification/Registration cards must be presented
upon entering the premises and must be worn while inside the Institute premises.
b) Identification/Registration cards are not transferable. The owner of the card
will be called upon to account for any fraudulent use of his/her identification card
and will be subject to disciplinary action if he/she has aided such fraudulent use.
The card will be forfeited if the student to whom it was issued allows any other
person to use the same.
c) Upon card owner’s withdrawal/rustication/expulsion from the Institute, all
rights and privileges related to the identification card automatically cease. Upon
withdrawal, or suspension or expulsion from the Institute, the identification card
must be surrendered to the Institute.
d) No visitors and guests are allowed to enter in the Institute premises or at
Institute-sponsored activities without the written permission of responsible authority.
e) All permitted/allowed visitors or invitees shall conduct themselves, at all
times, in a manner which is consistent and in line with maintenance of orderly
behavior in the Institute premises.
Students Discipline Committee
7. The Students Discipline Committee will comprise of members i.e, Registrar, Dean
or HoD or a senior faculty member, Director/Deputy Director Administration, GM Security,
College Director and Campus Director. One of these members will be nominated as
Chairperson by the President PAF-KIET. The quorum for a meeting of the Committee shall
be at least three members.
8. The function of the Committee shall be:-
a) To propose Regulations for the conduct of the Institute students.
b) To maintain discipline and to guard against any breach of discipline.
c) To Carry out investigations and take decisions of the breach of discipline
cases.
d) To Carry out investigations and put up recommendations of the cases
referred by president.
e) To perform such other functions as may be prescribed.
9. A student shall be guilty of an act of indiscipline and shall be liable for each act to
one or more of the penalties mentioned at Para-10, if he:
a) Commits a breach of any of the code of conduct specified at para-4 above; or
b. Disobeys the lawful order of a teacher or other persons in authority in the
Institute; or
c. Habitually neglects his work or habitually abstains himself from his class
without reasonable cause; or
d. Willfully damages Institute property or the property of a fellow student or any
teacher or any employee of the Institute; or
e. Does not comply with the rules relating to dress code; or
f. Uses indecent language, wears un modest dress or makes indecent remarks
or gestures or behavior; or
g. Does not pay the fees, fines or other dues liable, under the Institute Act,
Statutes, Rules, Regulations or Instructions; or
h. Commits any criminal, immoral or dishonorable act (whether committed
within Institute campus or otherwise) which is prejudicial to the interests of the
Institute; or
j. Humiliates, or causes to humiliate, misbehaves, uses abusive language or
manhandles his fellow student or a teacher or officer or other employees of the
Institute; or
k. Possesses, carries or uses any type of weapons/fire arms or explosive
material within the Institute premises; or
l. Spreads by word, mouth or written material, political, religious, sectarian,
ethnic, regional or linguistic conflicts/hatred; or
m. Unauthorized uses or takes possession of the Institute transport; or
n. Shows un modest/indecent or contra-Islamic behavior with fellow students.
