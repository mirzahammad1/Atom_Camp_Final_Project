QEC Policy
Quality Assurance Policy
Background
The Karachi Institute of Economics and Technology (KIET) is currently navigating a major institutional transition following the termination of its collaborative partnership with the Pakistan Air Force (PAF). This separation marks a significant shift in the universities operational and governance landscape, as the joint venture previously formed the foundation of shared policy-making, infrastructure, and human resource management.
As a result of this disengagement, KIET has been compelled to forfeit several critical resources, including access to premises and infrastructure, leading to the relocation of campuses and the need for extensive redevelopment. The dissolution has also led to a shortfall in human resources, creating challenges in academic administration and strategic implementation. Additionally, the process of segregating institutional data and redefining governance protocols has added further complexity to the transition.
Despite these considerable obstacles, KIET stands resolute in its mission to uphold academic integrity and ensure an uninterrupted teaching and learning experience for students. The institution’s foremost priority remains the delivery of quality education and the achievement of learning outcomes, without compromise.
This period of change has also provided an opportunity to reimagine and restructure the university’s operational model, realigning it with national quality standards and global best practices. With a focused approach to rebuilding its institutional identity—crafted over 25 years—KIET is leveraging the strength of its committed leadership, dedicated faculty, and supportive stakeholders.
Through the active cooperation of higher authorities and regulatory bodies, KIET continues to maintain momentum toward institutional resilience, renewal, and academic excellence. This transformation is not only a testament to KIET’s adaptability but also to its enduring vision for providing future-ready education.
Introduction
In today’s dynamic landscape, the Higher Education System in Pakistan is undergoing significant evolution—expanding with purpose and embracing the critical role of learning outcomes in quality assurance frameworks, academic performance metrics, and institutional audits. Clearly articulated objectives are now being prioritized to achieve meaningful outcomes. These not only offer transparent educational value to students, employers, and stakeholders but also drive continuous improvement in program monitoring, curriculum development, teaching methodologies, and overall academic delivery.
Such efforts directly support program accreditations, elevate institutional standings in quality rankings, and strengthen public and stakeholder confidence in institutional performance. The establishment of measurable learning outcomes and structured processes allows for greater accountability and control in areas that were once considered intangible within academic quality assurance.
To ensure consistency and alignment, policy guidelines are being developed to regulate these processes systematically. These
Institutional Quality Assurance Guidelines
are designed to align KIET’s Quality Policy with both national and international standards and best practices, in full compliance with the
Pakistan Standards and Guidelines for Quality Assurance in Higher Education (PSG-2023)
as issued by the Higher Education Commission of Pakistan.
Aim
The Quality Assurance Policy of KIET aims to foster a robust culture of quality in teaching, learning, and research, enhance the effectiveness and impact of academic and research outputs, and support the implementation of national and international quality standards across all institutional processes.
Vision
To achieve and sustain excellence in education at KIET through continuous quality improvement, adoption of recommended best practices, and implementation of HEC recognized quality standards.
Mission
The Directorate of Quality Enhancement (DQE) at KIET is committed to the continuous improvement of academic and administrative processes by promoting a culture of quality-centered education. The mission is to ensure stakeholder satisfaction, uphold the integrity of academic standards, and strengthen public confidence in the quality and credibility of the degrees awarded by the institution.
Quality Assurance Policy Statement
KIET is dedicated to promoting a dynamic quality culture in higher education and research, driven by a self-assessment and continuous improvement approach. The policy seeks to align all academic and administrative functions with the kiet’s core mission and vision, ensuring adherence to national standards and international best practices in higher education quality assurance.
Core Pillars of KIET’s Quality Assurance Policy
Compliance with Statutory Requirements
KIET ensures that all individuals associated with the institute —whether faculty, staff, or students—adhere strictly to the kiets ’s Charter, Statutes, Rules, Regulations, and Institutional Policies in letter and spirit.
Core Principle of the Quality Assurance Policy
KIET is committed to serving the nation and addressing the evolving needs of local then global markets by delivering high-quality higher education. This is achieved through an efficient and effective quality assurance system anchored in academic excellence, research innovation, and continuous quality improvement. The QA policy is built on a holistic, self-assessment-based approach( i )that encourages ethical development and prepares students to be responsible and impactful citizens.

Student-Centered Approach
recognizing students as the primary stakeholders in the educational process, KIET is dedicated to producing competent, skilled graduates across multidisciplinary domains. The institute strives to meet students’ academic and professional aspirations while valuing their input in fostering a dynamic and progressive learning environment.
Stakeholder Engagement
All stakeholders—academic, industrial, Governmental, and societal—are integral to KIET’s mission. It cultivates an inclusive, empowering, and collaborative atmosphere that supports knowledge creation, innovation, and societal impact for the collective benefit of all.
Knowledge Creation and Dissemination
KIET actively promotes multidisciplinary research aimed at addressing key socio-economic, political, technological, and environmental challenges. This knowledge is disseminated through seminars, workshops, conferences, and scholarly publications in collaboration with national and international partners.
Support for Startups and Innovation
KIET encourages innovation and entrepreneurship by supporting startups and business ventures across diverse academic fields, providing a launchpad for students and researchers to transform ideas into impactful enterprises.
Strategic Linkages and Collaborations
KIET maintains robust linkages with national and international higher education institutions, industry partners, quality assurance networks, (PNQAHE), and ( INQAAHE, APQN and accreditation councils. These partnerships are pursued through formal coordination and authorized channels to ensure relevance, recognition, and quality alignment.
Resource Optimization
KIET emphasizes the optimal utilization of human capital, infrastructure, and financial resources to ensure sustainable growth and maximize institutional effectiveness and efficiency.
Environmentally Sustainable Campus
KIET is committed to fostering a clean, green, safe, and stimulating campus environment. The university promotes environmental responsibility and offers an atmosphere conducive to intellectual growth, excellence, and personal development for faculty, staff, and students.
