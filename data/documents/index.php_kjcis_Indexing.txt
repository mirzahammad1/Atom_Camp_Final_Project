Abstracting and Indexing
Current abstracting & indexing
KJCIS is committed to ensuring the discoverability and long-term accessibility of published research. The journal is currently indexed and abstracted in national and international services, and metadata for all published articles is deposited with relevant indexing services.
Indexing (examples included as journal policy statements):
CrossRef (DOI registration and metadata deposition)
WorldCat
Directory of Research Journals
EuroPub
Ongoing indexing goals
KJCIS actively pursues further indexing in major international databases, including DOAJ and Scopus. To meet the requirements of these services, the journal maintains transparent editorial policies, rigorous peer-review procedures, regular publication schedules, and consistent metadata practices.
Metadata & discoverability
All articles are assigned CrossRef DOIs with complete metadata submissions.
Article pages include English titles, English abstracts, author affiliations, ORCID (when provided), and structured keywords.
Machine-readable metadata (schema.org / Dublin Core) and an OAI-PMH endpoint are used to facilitate harvesting by aggregators.
Indexing readiness & compliance
The journal follows best practices required by major indexers:
Clear aims and scope in English
Full editorial board with institutional affiliations
Detailed peer-review and ethics policies publicly available
Timely publication and accessible archive of past issues
Open access policy and transparent business model
DOI registration and timely metadata deposits
How to cite KJCIS articles
Citation format includes Article Title, Author(s), KJCIS, Volume(Issue), Page range, Year, and DOI. Each article page provides citation export options (RIS / BibTeX).
Indexing enquiries
For enquiries about indexing, metadata, or deposit requests, contact the journal's editorial office. Provide the article DOI, issue details, and the indexing service targeted.
