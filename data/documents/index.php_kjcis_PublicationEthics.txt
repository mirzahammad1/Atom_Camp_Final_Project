Publication Ethics and Misconduct Policy
KJCIS is committed to the highest standards of publication ethics and follows internationally recognized guidelines for handling allegations of misconduct. This policy applies to authors, reviewers, editors, and the publisher.
Core ethical principles
Honesty and accuracy in reporting research methods and results.
Originality and proper attribution of others’ work.
Transparency in authorship, contributions, funding, and conflicts of interest.
Confidential and impartial peer review.
Definition of misconduct
Misconduct includes, but is not limited to:
Plagiarism, including self-plagiarism and redundant publication.
Data fabrication or falsification.
Image manipulation that misleads readers.
Undisclosed conflicts of interest.
Authorship malpractices (ghost authorship, gift authorship).
Compromised or fake peer review.
Detection & investigation process
Identification:
Allegations can originate from editors, reviewers, readers, or external bodies.
Preliminary assessment:
The editorial office performs an initial assessment to determine if the allegation has substance.
Investigation:
If substantive, the editorial office will assemble evidence and may consult independent experts. The journal may request explanations from the authors and, if necessary, ask the authors’ institutions to conduct formal investigations.
Decision:
Based on the evidence and institutional findings, the editorial office will determine corrective actions (correction, expression of concern, or retraction).
Possible outcomes & sanctions
Correction:
For honest errors that do not affect the overall validity of the work.
Expression of Concern:
If an investigation is ongoing and there is reason to alert readers.
Retraction:
For research that is unreliable due to misconduct or serious error, or when ethical breaches are confirmed.
Editor’s note:
Clarifications or editorial comments to contextualize findings.
Bans:
In cases of proven misconduct, authors may be barred from submitting for a defined period.
Retraction & correction notices
Retraction notices will be linked to the original article, clearly state the reasons for retraction, and identify who is retracting the article. Corrections and expressions of concern will similarly be linked and transparent.
Cooperation with institutions & third parties
The journal may request that authors’ institutions investigate allegations. KJCIS will cooperate fully with institutional investigations and will act on the findings.
How to report suspected misconduct
Reports should be sent in writing to the Editorial Office with supporting evidence (e.g., similarity reports, data anomalies). The journal will acknowledge receipt and, where appropriate, keep the complainant informed about the progress of the investigation while respecting confidentiality.
Appeals
Authors or complainants may appeal findings or sanctions by submitting a written appeal to the Editor-in-Chief with new evidence. Appeals will be reviewed impartially.
Promoting publication integrity
The journal promotes integrity via clear policies, reviewer training, plagiarism screening, data availability requirements, and editorial oversight. KJCIS supports COPE guidelines and best practices for handling publication ethics.
