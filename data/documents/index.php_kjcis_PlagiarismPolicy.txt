Plagiarism / Misconduct Policy
KJCIS maintains a strict zero-tolerance policy toward plagiarism, data manipulation, unethical authorship practices, and all forms of research misconduct. All submissions are screened using similarity detection software before entering the peer-review process.
Similarity Check
Manuscripts are checked via Turnitin or equivalent tools.
Similarity index must comply with national guidelines (e.g., <19% as per HEC standards).
Excessive similarity or uncited overlap leads to immediate rejection or revision request.
Forms of Plagiarism
Direct plagiarism:
Copying text without citation.
Self-plagiarism:
Reusing previously published work without disclosure.
Patchwriting:
Rewriting another’s text with minor modifications.
Redundant or duplicate publication.
Unattributed paraphrasing.
Research Misconduct
Fabrication or falsification of data.
Manipulation of images, graphs, or results.
Submitting the same article to multiple journals simultaneously.
Unethical authorship practices (ghost, gift, or forged authorship).
Compromised, fake, or manipulated peer-review.
Handling Suspected Misconduct
Initial investigation by editorial office.
Request explanation or raw data from authors.
Consultation with independent experts if needed.
Decision based on evidence — correction, revision, rejection, or retraction.
Serious cases may be referred to author institutions.
Possible Outcomes
Request for correction or revision.
Editorial rejection at any stage.
Retraction of published articles.
Author ban (temporary or permanent).
Notification to institutions or funders.
Responsibilities of Authors, Reviewers & Editors
All parties must adhere to ethical standards and avoid any behavior that compromises integrity. The journal follows COPE guidelines for ethical publication.
