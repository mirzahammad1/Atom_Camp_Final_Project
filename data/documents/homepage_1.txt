1. Introduction
1.1 Scope of this Document
This document provides the description of BE (Electrical) program offered by College of Engineering
at Karachi Institute of Economics and Technology. This includes the program introduction, program
structure, scheme of studies and description of courses taught in the program.
1.2 Program Objectives
The objectives of BE (Electrical Engineering) program are to prepare electrical engineers:
1. With a sound knowledge of principles of mathematics, science, fundamentals of engineering design and
analysis, and professional ethics
2. Who are able to work in a global and diversified society
3. Who meet the needs of industry for electrical engineering or to pursue graduate studies.
4. Who are able to communicate clearly in written and oral formats.
5.
1.3 Program Composition
Electrical Engineering Department has come up with a comprehensive curriculum covering not only the
areas of engineering and sciences but also incorporating courses in humanities, social sciences and
management sciences. This is because Dept of EE wants to make visionary engineers with high ethical and
moral values working to serve the humanity.
BE (Electrical) is a 137 credit hours program. Courses have been classified as non-engineering and
engineering courses as shown in the table below.
Number of Courses Credit Hours
Non – Engineering 15 41
Engineering 29*/30 96
Total 43*/44 137
* For specialization in computer engineering
1
The curriculum has been designed to provide the student basic knowledge in the broader area of electrical
engineering. The course of study is focused on the fundamental concepts enabling the students to pursue a
career in any area from research and development to sales. In addition to the laboratory work that get the
student familiar with electrical, electronic and computing equipment and with experimental techniques,
course projects in almost every course help the student to learn how theoretical knowledge can be converted
into working projects.
The curriculum is designed in a way that the first three to four semesters cover the courses of general interest
(Engineering Foundation). This is followed by covering the electrical engineering breadth in the third year.
The final year include elective courses allowing the student to specialize either in Electronic Engineering,
Telecommunication Engineering or Computer Systems Engineering. A two-semester senior design project
usually involving industrial collaboration provides students with valuable experience in applying their
knowledge and skills for solution of real-world problems.

1. Scope of this Document
This document provides the description of BE (Avionics) program offered by College of Engineering at
Karachi Institute of Economics and Technology. This includes the program introduction, program
structure, scheme of studies and description of courses taught in the program.
2. Introduction
Program Objectives
The objectives of BE (Avionic Engineering) program are to prepare avionic engineers:
1. With a sound knowledge of principles of mathematics, science, fundamentals of engineering design
and analysis, and professional ethics
2. Who are able to work in a global and diversified society
3. Who meet the needs of industry for Avionic Engineering or to pursue graduate studies?
4. And who are also able to communicate clearly in written and oral formats.
Program Composition
CoE has come up with a comprehensive curriculum covering not only the areas of engineering and sciences
but also incorporating courses in humanities, social sciences and management sciences. This is because
CoE wants to make visionary engineers with high ethical and moral values working to serve the humanity.
BE (Avionics) is a 134 credit hours program excluding the 6 credit hours of Sports and Internships. Courses
have been classified as non-engineering and engineering courses as shown in the table below.
Number of Courses Credit Hours
Non – Engineering 16 41
Engineering 30 93
Total 46 134
1
The curriculum has been designed to provide the student basic knowledge in the broader area of Electrical
Engineering which is followed by focused courses related to Avionic Engineering. The course of study is
focused on the fundamental concepts enabling the students to pursue a career in any area from research
and development to sales. In addition to the laboratory work that get the student familiar with electrical,
electronic and computing equipment and with experimental techniques, course projects in almost every
course help the student to learn how theoretical knowledge can be converted into working projects.
The curriculum is designed in a way that the first three to four semesters cover the courses of general
interest (Engineering Foundation). This is followed by covering the Avionic Engineering breadth starting
from the third year and extending into fifth and sixth semesters. The senior semesters from fifth till eighth
semester include depth courses allowing the student to specialize in Avionic Engineering. A two-semester
senior design project usually involving industrial collaboration provides students with valuable experience
in applying their knowledge and skills for solution of real-world problems.

The curriculum has been designed to provide the student basic knowledge in the broad area of
Mechatronic engineering. The course of study is focused on the fundamental concepts enabling
the students to pursue a career in any area from research and development to sales. In addition to
the laboratory work that makes the student familiar with electrical, electronic and computing
equipment and with experimental techniques, course projects, in almost every course, help the
student to learn how theoretical knowledge can be converted into working projects.
The curriculum is designed in a way that the first three to four semesters cover the courses of
general interest (Engineering Foundation). This is followed by covering the electrical engineering
breadth in the third year. The final year includes elective courses allowing the student to specialize
Mechatronic Engineering. A two-semester senior design project usually involving industrial
collaboration provides students with valuable experience in applying their knowledge and skills
for solution of real-world problems.

1. Introduction
1.1. Scope of this Document
This document provides the description of the BE (Software Engineering) program offered by the
College of Engineering at Karachi Institute of Economics and Technology. This includes the program
introduction, program structure, scheme of studies and description of courses taught in the program.
1.2. Vision of Software Engineering Program
The vision statement of the Software Engineering program is as follows
“To impart highest quality engineering education with an aim to produce proficient, creative and
ethical software engineers who can take the role of visionary leaders, entrepreneurs and researchers at
national and global levels”
The above vision was approved by the ACM held on 18th March, 2019
1.3. Mission of Software Engineering Program
The mission statement of the Software Engineering program is as follows
“To educate students through highly qualified faculty to have strong theoretical and practical
expertise, team-work, leadership qualities, ethical values and entrepreneurship skills. Establish and
maintain state-of-the-art laboratories, latest curriculum and industrial linkages. Prepare graduates to
pursue higher studies and undertake research at national and global levels. ”
The above mission was approved by the ACM held on 18th March, 2019
1.4. Program Education Objectives (PEOs)
There are three Program Education objectives of BE (Software Engineering) program:
1. PEO1: The graduates will be able to apply software engineering knowledge to critically
analyze, investigate and design innovative solutions for complex technological problems.
2. PEO2: The graduates will be able to demonstrate sustained career development through life
long learning, ethical practices and effective communication skills.
3. PEO3: The graduates will be able to use their skills by undertaking roles as entrepreneurs and
project managers to undertake new technological projects for development of society.

areas of engineering and sciences but also incorporating courses in humanities, social sciences and

moral values working to serve the humanity.
