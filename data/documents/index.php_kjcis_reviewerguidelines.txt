Reviewer Guidelines
Overview
Reviewers play a vital role in maintaining the quality of scholarship published in KJCIS. This page outlines responsibilities, review criteria, confidentiality expectations, and the logistical process for peer review.
Role & responsibilities
Provide an objective, constructive, and timely review of assigned manuscripts.
Assess novelty, methodological rigor, clarity, reproducibility, and ethical compliance.
Identify strengths and weaknesses and provide specific recommendations for improvement.
Declare any conflicts of interest and recuse if impartiality cannot be guaranteed.
Confidentiality
Manuscripts and related materials are confidential and must not be shared or used for the reviewer’s personal advantage. The identity of authors should not be disclosed.
Review criteria & questions
When preparing your review, please address the following:
Scope & relevance:
Is the manuscript appropriate for KJCIS and its audience?
Originality & novelty:
Does it present new findings, methods, or significant synthesis?
Technical quality:
Is the methodology sound and adequately described?
Validity of results:
Are experiments or analyses correctly performed and interpreted?
Clarity & organization:
Is the article well-structured and clearly written?
Ethical compliance:
Are ethical approvals and data declarations included where required?
References:
Are citations appropriate and sufficiently comprehensive?
Recommendation scale
Use the following recommendation categories in your review:
Accept
Minor revision
Major revision
Reject
Review format & constructive feedback
Provide a short confidential summary for the editor and detailed comments for the author. Prioritize constructive, actionable suggestions and reference specific line numbers when possible.
Timelines
Typical turnaround: 2–4 weeks. If reviewers cannot meet the deadline, they should inform the editor promptly to allow alternative arrangements.
Conflicts of interest
Declare financial, institutional, or personal conflicts. If a conflict exists, decline the review.
Reviewer form (template)
The reviewer form includes: summary comments, strengths, weaknesses, specific line-by-line suggestions, recommendation, and confidentiality notes to the editor. Download the reviewer form from the journal’s resources page.
Recognition & responsibilities
KJCIS acknowledges reviewers’ contributions annually and may provide certificates of recognition. Consistent and high-quality reviewing may be considered for editorial roles.
