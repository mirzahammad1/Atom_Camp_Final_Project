Copyright / Licensing Policy
This page explains KJCIS’s approach to copyright ownership, licensing, permissions, and reuse.
Copyright ownership
Authors retain copyright of their published work. Upon acceptance, authors grant KJCIS a non-exclusive license to publish and distribute the article in print and online formats.
Open license (recommended)
KJCIS publishes articles under the
Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0)
license. This license permits unrestricted reuse, distribution, and reproduction in any medium, provided the original work is properly cited. Where specific funding or institutional mandates require a different license, authors should inform the editorial office at submission.
Permissions for third-party material
Authors must obtain written permission for any third-party material (figures, tables, long text extracts) and provide evidence of permission during submission. Third-party material must be clearly credited and labeled in the manuscript.
Self-archiving & author rights
Authors may self-archive preprints, accepted manuscripts, and published versions subject to the CC BY license terms. Authors should include citation and DOI when depositing to repositories.
License changes & versioning
The journal may update licensing policies; such changes will be announced. Licensing for previously published articles remains as stated at the time of publication unless otherwise agreed with the authors.
Attribution examples
Proper citation should include: Author(s), Article title, KJCIS, Volume(Issue), Page range, Year, DOI.
Contact for permissions and queries
For permission requests, licensing clarifications, or copyright issues, contact the Editorial Office with article details and the specific request.
